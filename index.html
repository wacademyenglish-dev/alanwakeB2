THE NARRATIVE: PART I

PAST SIMPLE & CONTINUOUS

Setting the Scene vs. Sudden Action

We use Past Continuous to set the background scene (an action in progress), and Past Simple for the sudden action that interrupts it.

While Alan was typing, the shadow broke the window.

THE NARRATIVE: PART II

PAST PERFECT

The Past of the Past

Use it when you are telling a story in the past, and need to refer to an action that happened even earlier.

When Saga arrived at the cabin, Alan had already vanished.
